# Media SEO

Media websites earn visibility by consistently publishing authoritative, timely, and engaging content.

SEO should maximize discoverability while encouraging readers to continue exploring related topics.

Editorial quality should always outweigh publishing volume.

---

# Search Intent

Visitors typically search to:

- Read news
- Learn about current events
- Research topics
- Follow industries
- Discover expert opinions

---

# Primary Keyword Categories

- News
- Current events
- Industry topics
- Editorial content
- Analysis
- Interviews

Prioritize topical authority over isolated articles.

---

# Essential Landing Pages

Required

- Home
- Categories
- Articles
- Search
- Contact

Recommended

- Authors
- Newsletter
- Podcasts
- Videos
- Opinion

---

# Information Architecture

Content should be organized around topics rather than publication dates.

Readers should naturally discover related articles.

---

# Internal Linking Strategy

Connect:

- Related articles
- Categories
- Authors
- Series
- Evergreen content

Build strong topical clusters.

---

# Structured Data

Required

- NewsArticle or Article
- Organization
- Website
- BreadcrumbList

Recommended

- Person
- VideoObject
- FAQPage

---

# Content Strategy

Publish:

- Breaking news
- Editorial analysis
- Industry insights
- Interviews
- Evergreen resources

Balance timely content with long-lasting authority.

---

# Local SEO

Generally unnecessary unless serving a regional audience.

---

# Trust Signals

High Priority

- Named authors
- Publication dates
- Sources
- Editorial standards

Medium Priority

- Awards
- Expert contributors
- Citations

---

# Technical Considerations

- Fast loading
- Article metadata
- Structured data
- Mobile-first
- Canonical URLs

---

# Common Mistakes

- Clickbait
- Thin articles
- Weak topic organization
- Duplicate coverage
- Poor internal linking

---

# Success Metrics

- Organic traffic
- Returning readers

# Healthcare SEO

Healthcare websites build visibility by providing accurate, trustworthy, and patient-focused information.

SEO should prioritize education, accessibility, and credibility over aggressive marketing.

Medical content should always support informed decision-making rather than encourage unnecessary treatments.

---

# Search Intent

Visitors typically search to:

- Understand symptoms
- Learn about conditions
- Find treatments
- Locate healthcare providers
- Book appointments

---

# Primary Keyword Categories

- Medical conditions
- Symptoms
- Treatments
- Specialties
- Procedures
- Healthcare providers
- Locations

Prioritize patient intent over search volume.

---

# Essential Landing Pages

Required

- Home
- About
- Services
- Doctors
- Contact

Recommended

- Conditions
- Treatments
- Patient Resources
- FAQs
- Locations
- Blog

---

# Information Architecture

Medical services should be organized logically by specialty.

Educational content should support treatment and provider pages.

Patients should easily locate appointment information.

---

# Internal Linking Strategy

Connect:

- Conditions
- Treatments
- Specialists
- Resources
- Appointment pages

Educational content should naturally guide patients toward appropriate care.

---

# Structured Data

Required

- Organization
- MedicalOrganization
- Physician
- Website
- BreadcrumbList

Recommended

- FAQPage

---

# Content Strategy

Create medically accurate content including:

- Condition guides
- Treatment explanations
- Preventive care
- Frequently asked questions
- Patient education

Medical information should be reviewed by qualified professionals whenever appropriate.

---

# Local SEO

Critical.

Include:

- Clinic locations
- Contact information
- Operating hours
- Geographic service areas

---

# Trust Signals

High Priority

- Medical professionals
- Credentials
- Certifications
- Patient testimonials (where appropriate)
- Hospital affiliations

Medium Priority

- Awards
- Research
- Publications

---

# Technical Considerations

- Fast loading
- Accessibility
- Mobile-first
- Secure website
- Structured data
- Strong metadata

---

# Common Mistakes

- Misleading medical claims
- Thin medical content
- Anonymous authors
- Outdated information
- Weak trust signals

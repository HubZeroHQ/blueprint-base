# Nonprofit SEO

Nonprofit websites grow through trust, transparency, and meaningful storytelling.

SEO should help supporters understand the organization's mission while encouraging long-term engagement.

Mission should always come before fundraising.

---

# Search Intent

Visitors typically search to:

- Learn about the mission
- Donate
- Volunteer
- Attend events
- Find programs

---

# Primary Keyword Categories

- Mission
- Programs
- Causes
- Donations
- Volunteering
- Community initiatives

---

# Essential Landing Pages

Required

- Home
- About
- Programs
- Donate
- Contact

Recommended

- Events
- Volunteer
- Stories
- Impact
- News

---

# Information Architecture

Programs should be easy to explore.

Donation and volunteer opportunities should remain easily accessible throughout the website.

---

# Internal Linking Strategy

Connect:

- Programs
- Stories
- Events
- Donate
- Volunteer

Success stories should naturally support fundraising pages.

---

# Structured Data

Required

- NGO
- Organization
- Website
- BreadcrumbList

Recommended

- Event
- FAQPage
- Article

---

# Content Strategy

Publish:

- Impact reports
- Success stories
- Community updates
- Volunteer experiences
- Annual reports

Authenticity is more valuable than publishing frequency.

---

# Local SEO

Useful for community-focused organizations.

Include service areas, offices, and event locations.

---

# Trust Signals

High Priority

- Financial transparency
- Annual reports
- Impact metrics
- Partnerships
- Testimonials

Medium Priority

- Awards
- Certifications
- Media coverage

---

# Technical Considerations

- Fast loading
- Accessibility
- Secure donation flows
- Structured data
- Mobile-first

---

# Common Mistakes

- Unclear mission
- Weak donation experience
- Hidden impact information
- Poor transparency
- Outdated events

---

# Success Metrics

- Donations
- Volunteer registrations

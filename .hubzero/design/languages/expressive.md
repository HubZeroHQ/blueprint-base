# Expressive

Expressive design communicates personality, creativity, and originality.

Every visual decision should strengthen the brand's identity while preserving clarity and usability.

Originality should emerge from thoughtful design rather than novelty alone.

---

# Core Principles

- Personality over convention
- Creative confidence
- Memorable experiences
- Strong visual storytelling
- Purposeful originality

---

# Typography

Typography should become a defining part of the visual identity.

Hierarchy may be unconventional while remaining understandable.

---

# Layout

Layouts may intentionally break conventional patterns to create memorable experiences.

Visual experimentation should never reduce usability.

---

# Color Philosophy

Color should reinforce personality and emotion.

Palettes may be bold but should remain cohesive.

---

# Shapes

Shapes may vary significantly to support the brand's unique identity.

Consistency remains essential.

---

# Spacing

Spacing should support visual rhythm rather than strict uniformity.

Variation should feel intentional.

---

# Motion

Motion should contribute to storytelling and personality.

Animations should enhance the experience without distracting from content.

---

# Imagery

Illustrations, photography, graphics, and visual storytelling should reinforce the brand's creative identity.

Authenticity should always outweigh trendiness.

---

# Components

Components should express personality while remaining intuitive and accessible.

Creativity should never compromise usability.

---

# Suitable Architectures

- Creative Agencies
- Portfolio
- Marketing
- Media
- Community

---

# Avoid

Avoid experimentation that harms readability, accessibility, performance, or long-term maintainability.

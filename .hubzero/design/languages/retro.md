# Retro

Retro design draws inspiration from past visual eras while remaining modern in usability.

It should celebrate nostalgia without compromising accessibility, clarity, or performance.

Historical influence should feel intentional rather than theatrical.

---

# Core Principles

- Nostalgia with purpose
- Personality over perfection
- Authentic references
- Modern usability
- Memorable visual identity

---

# Typography

Typography may reference historical styles while remaining highly readable.

Avoid sacrificing clarity for authenticity.

---

# Layout

Layouts should balance vintage inspiration with contemporary usability.

Users should never struggle because of stylistic choices.

---

# Color Philosophy

Colors should evoke a specific historical mood rather than imitate it literally.

Palettes should remain cohesive and restrained.

---

# Shapes

Shapes may reference historical design systems while maintaining consistency.

Visual motifs should support the experience rather than dominate it.

---

# Spacing

Spacing should remain modern and readable even when visual inspiration is historical.

---

# Motion

Motion should feel playful but restrained.

Avoid exaggerated nostalgic effects.

---

# Imagery

Illustrations, photography, textures, and graphics should reinforce the chosen era authentically.

Avoid visual clichés.

---

# Components

Components should maintain modern interaction patterns despite their visual inspiration.

Usability always comes before nostalgia.

---

# Suitable Architectures

- Portfolio
- Hospitality
- Restaurants
- Creative Agencies
- Community

---

# Avoid

Avoid excessive decorative effects, artificial aging, or sacrificing usability for stylistic authenticity.

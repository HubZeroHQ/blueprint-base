# Industrial

Industrial design communicates strength, reliability, and engineering capability.

Interfaces should feel practical, durable, and purpose-built.

Visual decisions should reinforce competence rather than sophistication.

---

# Core Principles

- Function first
- Structural clarity
- Reliability
- Engineering confidence
- Practical communication

---

# Typography

Typography should be bold, highly legible, and structured.

Readability should always take priority.

---

# Layout

Layouts should feel organized and efficient.

Visual structure should communicate stability.

---

# Color Philosophy

Colors should reinforce reliability and professionalism.

Accent colors should communicate actions rather than decoration.

---

# Shapes

Strong geometry.

Moderate radius.

Consistent proportions.

---

# Spacing

Spacing should support efficient scanning and organized information.

Avoid unnecessary visual complexity.

---

# Motion

Motion should remain subtle and functional.

Animations should reinforce usability rather than aesthetics.

---

# Imagery

Factories.

Equipment.

Infrastructure.

People performing real work.

Engineering diagrams.

---

# Components

Components should emphasize clarity, durability, and efficiency.

Every interaction should feel dependable.

---

# Suitable Architectures

- Manufacturing
- Logistics
- Corporate
- Automotive
- Government

---

# Avoid

Avoid decorative effects, playful interactions, luxury aesthetics, or excessive visual experimentation.

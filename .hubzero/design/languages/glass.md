# Glass

Glass design communicates depth, lightness, and sophistication through layered interfaces.

Transparency should improve visual hierarchy rather than become a decorative effect.

Depth should always serve usability.

---

# Core Principles

- Layered interfaces
- Visual depth
- Subtle transparency
- Modern refinement
- Clarity through separation

---

# Typography

Typography should remain highly legible against layered backgrounds.

Contrast should always take precedence over aesthetics.

---

# Layout

Layouts should use layering to establish hierarchy without increasing complexity.

Depth should reinforce organization.

---

# Color Philosophy

Transparency should complement color rather than replace it.

Color should remain restrained and purposeful.

---

# Shapes

Soft geometry.

Balanced proportions.

Smooth surfaces.

---

# Spacing

Generous spacing prevents layered interfaces from feeling crowded.

Whitespace enhances perceived depth.

---

# Motion

Motion should reinforce the illusion of layered surfaces.

Transitions should feel smooth and physically consistent.

---

# Imagery

Clean photography, abstract gradients, and subtle environmental visuals work well within layered compositions.

Avoid cluttered imagery.

---

# Components

Components should communicate depth without reducing readability or accessibility.

Transparency should never obscure important information.

---

# Suitable Architectures

- SaaS
- Premium Products
- Marketing
- AI Platforms
- Technology Companies

---

# Avoid

Avoid excessive blur, unnecessary transparency, poor contrast, or decorative layering that reduces usability.
